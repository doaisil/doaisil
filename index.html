EU Creates a Rulebook for Chemical Recycling

From Ambiguity to Actionable Rules

On July 8, the European Commission launched a public consultation on rules for calculating, verifying, and reporting recycled content in single-use plastic beverage bottles, with a specific focus on accommodating chemical recycling. This is a landmark development, as it provides a clear, official methodology for a technology that has been in a regulatory gray area. The rules will be based on a mass balance approach with a "fuel-use excluded" allocation, meaning that plastic waste converted to energy cannot be counted towards recycled content targets.

Investment Security and a New Software Need

By creating a technology-neutral, level playing field, these rules are designed to provide investment security for the advanced recycling sector. For software providers, this creates a new, urgent, and highly specific market need. Chemical recycling operators, plastics producers, and beverage companies will require sophisticated software to track feedstocks, perform the complex mass-balance calculations, manage third-party verification workflows, and generate auditable reports that comply with these new EU standards.
